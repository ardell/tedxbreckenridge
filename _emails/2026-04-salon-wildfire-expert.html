Seeing Through the Eyes of a Wildfire Expert

May 5, 2026 • Doors 5:30 PM • Talk 6:00 PM
Silverthorne Pavilion — Silverthorne, CO
Adults $10 · Kids (4+) $10

Get Tickets

What if the fires we fear are also essential to the health of the ecosystems we love?

How do experts balance letting fire do its work while keeping people safe?

And what does it really take to protect a mountain community from wildfire?

In this Salon, Hannah Ohlson — Fuels Specialist with Summit Fire & EMS — will take us inside the surprising science of wildfire: why forests evolved to burn, how fire shapes the ecology of our mountain communities, and what it means to live alongside it.

Before joining Summit Fire, Hannah served on U.S. Forest Service Hotshot and Helitack Crews and earned a Master's in Natural Resources Stewardship from Colorado State University. In 2026, she received the Wildfire Mitigation Award from the International Association of Fire Chiefs — the highest national honor for wildfire preparedness and mitigation work.

Expect an eye-opening talk followed by small-group discussion, where you'll connect with neighbors and fellow curious minds to ask questions, share perspectives, and rethink what wildfire means for the forests and communities we love.

Bring the whole family! Kids (ages 4+) can enjoy hands-on wildfire science activities by Keystone Science School and a fire truck tour with Summit Fire & EMS firefighters. A kids ticket is required — space is limited.
