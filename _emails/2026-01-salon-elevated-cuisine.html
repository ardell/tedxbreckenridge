Elevated Cuisine: The Science and Art of Cooking at Altitude

January 15, 2026 • 6:00 PM
EVO3 Workspace — Frisco, CO
Suggested donation: $10

Get Tickets

Why does water boil at a lower temperature in the mountains?

Why do cakes rise too fast—or not at all?

And how do professional chefs adapt when cooking at 9,000 feet?

In this Salon, the culinary team from Colorado Mountain College will explore how altitude changes the rules of cooking and baking — and what it takes to master flavor, texture, and technique in the High Rockies. Through a short talk, practical insights, and guided discussion, we'll dig into the science behind high-elevation kitchens and the creative adaptations that make great food possible up here.

Come ready to think, ask questions, and leave with ideas you can actually use in your own kitchen.

Space is limited. Salon events are intentionally small to keep the conversation intimate.
