Elevated Cuisine:
The Science and Art of Cooking at Altitude

Why does water boil at a lower temperature in the mountains? Why do cakes rise too fast—or not at all? And how do professional chefs adapt their techniques when cooking at 9,000 feet?

In this TEDxBreckenridge Salon, the culinary team from Colorado Mountain College explores how altitude changes the rules of cooking and baking—and what it takes to master flavor, texture, and technique in the High Rockies. Through a short talk, practical insights, and guided discussion, we'll dive into the science behind high-elevation kitchens and the creative adaptations that make great food possible up here.

Come ready to think, ask questions, and leave with ideas you'll actually use in your own kitchen.

About Salons

What is a TEDxBreckenridge Salon?

TEDxBreckenridge Salon events are intimate, conversational gatherings that bring together curious minds to explore fascinating topics. Unlike our main annual event, Salons are smaller by design—creating space for deeper discussion, questions, and genuine connection.

Each Salon features a short talk or presentation from a local expert, followed by guided conversation where everyone's voice matters. It's learning that feels less like a lecture and more like sitting around a table with friends who love to explore ideas together.

Topics vary throughout the year—from science and technology to art, culture, and community challenges. What stays constant is the spirit: curiosity-driven, accessible to all, and designed to leave you thinking differently about the world around you.
